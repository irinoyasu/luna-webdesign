# Business Model Phase Reference

## Purpose

Define the economic logic of the venture, including revenue streams and cost drivers. This phase ensures that the solution is not just a product, but a sustainable and scalable business.

## Inputs

**Process Dependencies:**
- **Phase 3: Solution (Mandatory):** Pricing model (Value Stick) and ARPU/conversion assumptions.
- **Phase 4: Technology (Mandatory):** Infrastructure scaling costs and R&D requirements.

**You MUST synthesize information from ALL previously completed phases before drafting.** 
The business plan is a cumulative pipeline. Your inputs include:
1. **Previous Phase Outputs:** All documents and research logs generated in Phases 1-4.
2. **Existing Workspace Data:** Always check the output directory (`{output_folder}/businessplan-writing/`) for any existing `business-model/` folder or data from preceding phases. If files already exist from a previous execution, you must incorporate their content to ensure continuity and avoid duplication.
3. **Primary Direct Dependencies:** Phase 3: Solution (pricing models and value propositions) and Phase 4: Technology (core technology costs and infrastructure needs).

## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Write Business Model Name

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 3: Solution:** Use the product name and core value proposition to define the model type.
- **Phase 1: Research:** Compare against industry-standard business models for similar product categories.

Identify and describe the business model:
- Classify the model type (e.g., SaaS, marketplace, platform, direct sales, franchise, subscription)
- Reference established business model patterns (Business Model Canvas, Lean Canvas)
- Explain why this model fits the problem/solution
- Compare with alternative models considered

### 2. Write Cost Structure

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 4: Technology:** Use the infrastructure requirements to estimate hosting and scaling costs.
- **Phase 10: Operations:** Use the operational headcount and facility requirements.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Fixed costs must be phased according to the release timing of product.
- **Phase 11: Team:** Use the recruitment plan and seniority mix to estimate payroll overhead.

Define all costs required to operate the business:

**Fixed Costs:**
- Office/facility rent
- Salaries and benefits (by department)
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Insurance and legal
- Equipment and depreciation

**Variable Costs:**
- Cost of goods sold (COGS)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Infrastructure scaling costs
- Transaction fees
- Commission and referral fees

**Key Metrics:**
- Gross margin target
- Operating margin target
- Unit economics (cost per customer, cost per transaction)
- Break-even analysis

### 3. Write Revenue Streams

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 3: Solution:** Use the finalized price points, tier structure, and Value Stick (WTP/Price) analysis.
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** Revenue targets must be consistent with the identified scale of the problem/customer segments.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Revenue generation start dates depend on the release timing of product.

Define all revenue sources:

**Primary Revenue:**
- Product/service sales
- Subscription fees
- Licensing fees
- Transaction/usage fees

**Secondary Revenue:**
- Consulting/professional services
- Training and certification
- Partnerships and referrals
- Data monetization (if applicable)

**For each stream, document:**
- Revenue model (one-time, recurring, usage-based)
- Pricing tiers and expected volumes
- Revenue growth projections (Year 1–5)
- Customer lifetime value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC ratio target
