## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Write Contrarian Hypothesis

Articulate the non-obvious insight that drives the venture and frame it as a testable business bet.

**Framework (inspired by Peter Thiel and Tim Herbig):**
- **Identify the Contrarian Truth:** What important truth do few people agree with you on? What does the conventional wisdom say, and why is it wrong?
- **Draft the If/Then Hypothesis:**
    - **If we:** [Specific action or solution based on your unique insight]
    - **For:** [Specific target persona identified in Phase 2]
    - **Then we will:** [Attain a measurable outcome or job-to-be-done that conventional approaches miss]
- **Design 'Tiny Acts of Discovery':** List 2-3 lightweight experiments to test this hypothesis (e.g., concierge test, clickable prototype, or landing page experiment).
- **Define Validation Measures:** Specify what quantitative and qualitative signals prove the hypothesis is valid within a 2-4 week timeframe.
- **Evidence of Conviction:** Describe the secret or insight discovered through research that supports this bet.

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 1: Research:** Use technical and market intelligence to identify industry "conventional wisdom."
- **Phase 8: Competition:** Map competitor weaknesses to identify strategic gaps for the hypothesis.
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** Ensure the hypothesis addresses the specific persona's unserved JTBD and opportunity cost.

**Deliverable:**
- `insight/contrarian-hypothesis.md` containing the contrarian narrative and the structured, testable hypothesis with validation experiments.

### 2. Write Why Now

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 7: Market:** Use PEST and growth trends to identify timing catalysts.
- **Phase 4: Technology:** Use technology maturity and cost shifts as a timing justification.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Timing catalysts must align with the release timing of product to capture the market window.

Explain the timing — why is this the right moment to start:

**Catalysts to examine:**
- Technology shifts (new capabilities, cost reductions, maturity)
- Market shifts (regulatory changes, customer behavior, market growth)
- Macro trends (economic, demographic, social, environmental)
- Competitive gaps (incumbent weakness, market consolidation, new entrants)
- Platform shifts (mobile, cloud, AI, blockchain, etc.)

**Structure:**
1. Identify 3–5 timing factors
2. For each: what changed, when, and what it enables
3. Explain the consequences of launching too early or too late
4. Connect timing factors to market opportunity

### 3. Write Why You

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 11: Team:** Use the founders' unique domain expertise, relationships, and track records.
- **Phase 1: Research:** Use the initial company background and founding story.

Explain why this team is uniquely positioned to win:

**Elements to cover:**
- Founder-market fit: personal experience, passion, domain expertise
- Unique insights: proprietary knowledge, relationships, or technology
- Track record: relevant achievements, previous ventures, industry experience
- Unfair advantages: exclusive access, data, partnerships, networks
- Team complementarity: how team members' skills combine uniquely
- Commitment: evidence of dedication (time invested, capital contributed, bridges burned)
