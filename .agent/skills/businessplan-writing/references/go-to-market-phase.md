# Go-To-Market (GTM) Phase Reference

## Purpose

Define the strategy for acquiring and retaining customers scalably. This phase translates the business model and unit economics into a concrete execution plan for marketing and sales.

## Inputs

**Process Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity (Mandatory):** Personas and Customer Journeys must be defined to ensure channel alignment.
- **Phase 5: Business Model (Mandatory):** Unit economics (LTV target, CAC budget) are required for channel prioritization.
- **Phase 8: Competition (Mandatory):** Differentiation strategy and win themes must be finalized.

**You MUST synthesize information from ALL previously completed phases before drafting.** 
The business plan is a cumulative pipeline. Your inputs include:
1. **Previous Phase Outputs:** All documents and research logs generated in Phases 1-8.
2. **Existing Workspace Data:** Always check the output directory (`{output_folder}/businessplan-writing/`) for any existing `go-to-market/` folder or data from preceding phases. If files already exist from a previous execution, you must incorporate their content to ensure continuity and avoid duplication.
3. **Primary Direct Dependencies:** Phase 3: Solution (value proposition), Phase 5: Business Model (unit economics), Phase 7: Market (segmentation), and Phase 8: Competition (differentiation strategy).

This phase combines Marketing and Sales strategies into a unified Go-To-Market approach.

## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Research GTM

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 7: Market:** Use market segmentation data to target research for specific channels.
- **Phase 8: Competition:** Use competitor differentiation to identify gaps in their acquisition tactics.

Use web search tools to gather GTM intelligence and benchmarks.

**Research objectives:**
- Identify standard CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) and LTV (Lifetime Value) benchmarks for the industry.
- Search for competitor GTM tactics (SEO keywords, social presence, ad copies, sales outreach methods).
- Find trending marketing channels and sales automation tools.
- Research industry-specific exhibitions, trade shows, and lead generation platforms.
- Locate case studies on successful GTM launches in the sector.

**Deliverable:**
- `go-to-market/research-log.md` containing search queries, URLs visited, and key notes per source.

### 2. Write Marketing Channel Strategies

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** Align channel selection with where the target personas spend time and how they experience pain.
- **Phase 3: Solution:** Use the "Win Themes" and "Playbook" messaging for channel content.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Align tactical plans with the release timing of product.

1. **Channel Overview:** Purpose, audience fit, cost structure.
2. **Tactical Plan:** Launch activities (first 90 days) and ongoing cadence.
3. **Success Metrics:** Channel-specific KPIs (reach, engagement, conversion).
4. **Channels to Consider:**
   - **Corporate Website:** SEO, conversion optimization (CRO), user journeys.
   - **Content & Blog:** Keyword strategy, thought leadership, content calendar.
   - **Paid Media (PPC):** Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, bid strategies.
   - **Social Media:** Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, YouTube, LINE.
   - **Email & Newsletters:** Segmentation, drip campaigns, nurturing.
   - **Events & Seminars:** Trade shows, webinars, registration flows.
   - **Partners & Agents:** Channel partners, commission structures.

### 3. Write Sales Channel Strategies

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** Ensures the sales script and battle cards address the specific root causes and JTBD identified.
- **Phase 3: Solution:** Use the pricing tiers and value quantification in sales negotiations.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Sales ramp-up must align with the release timing of product.

Define how the sales team will engage and close prospects. For each channel (Email, Phone, Field Sales, etc.), document:

1. **Process:** Prospecting → Qualification → Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Closing → Handoff.
2. **Enablement:** Sales decks, scripts, battle cards, objection handling.
3. **Tech Stack:** CRM, sales automation, dialers.
4. **Team Structure:** Roles (SDR, AE, AM), quotas, and territories.

### 4. Evaluate Channel-Product Fit

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 5: Business Model:** Use target LTV:CAC and gross margin requirements to evaluate channel viability.
- **Phase 6: Traction:** Use existing traffic/conversion data as the baseline for performance projects.

- **Unit Economics Analysis:** Project or calculate CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC, and Payback Period.
- **Customer Quality Assessment:** Analyze churn risk, expansion potential (NRR), and ICP fit by channel.
- **Scalability Evaluation:** Estimate volume potential (Magic Number), addressable volume, and CAC trends.
- **Strategic Decision:** Categorize each channel as **Scale Aggressively**, **Test & Optimize**, **Kill/Pause**, or **Invest to Learn (Strategic)**.
- **Deliverable:** Multi-channel evaluation matrix and prioritization report in `go-to-market/channel-evaluation.md`.

### 5. Design Referral & Viral Loops

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 3: Solution:** Product features must facilitate sharing or provide "viral" value.

Create strategies that leverage existing users to acquire new ones (Product-Led Growth).

- **Incentivized Referrals:** Define referral programs, credits, or rewards for both referrers and referees.
- **Viral Loops:** Design inherent virality (e.g., collaborative features, "Powered by" badges) where product usage naturally invites others.
- **Ambassador Programs:** Structure programs for power users to champion the product for exclusive benefits.
- **Deliverable:** Visual or narrative map of referral and viral loops in `go-to-market/viral-loops.md`.

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Sub-task 1 (Stakeholders):** Identify where target communities naturally congregate (e.g., Slack, Reddit, specific associations).
- **Phase 8: Competition:** Use the competitive advantage to position the brand as a thought leader within these communities.

Define how to reach audiences through trusted communities and key opinion leaders (KOLs).

- **Community-Led Growth:** Define engagement strategies for owned communities (Discord, Slack) or earned communities (Reddit, specialized forums).
- **Influencer/KOL Partnerships:** Identify key influencers in the space and design collaboration models (guest posts, webinars, affiliate).
- **Thought Leadership:** Plan Guest appearances on podcasts and interviews.
- **Deliverable:** Community and influencer roadmap in `go-to-market/community-influencers.md`.

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 2 (Marketing) & 3 (Sales):** The funnel must integrate the specific activities and handoff points from both strategies.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Use current conversion rates as the baseline for the funnel logic.

Map the integrated lead-to-revenue journey:

| Stage | Owner | Activities | Metrics | Target CR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Marketing | Ads, Content, PR | Impressions, Reach | - |
| **Interest** | Marketing | Website visits, Social engagement | Clicks, CTR | % |
| **Consideration** | Marketing | Content downloads, Webinar attendance | MQL count | % |
| **Intent** | Sales (SDR) | Demo requests, Outbound replies | SQL count | % |
| **Evaluation** | Sales (AE) | Demos, Scoping, Trials | Opportunity count | % |
| **Negotiation** | Sales (AE) | Proposals, Contract review | Quote value | % |
| **Closed Won** | Sales/CS | Signature, Onboarding | New Revenue / Logos | % |

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 7 (Funnel):** KPIs must monitor the conversion health of each funnel stage.
- **Phase 5: Business Model:** Align KPIs with the target LTV:CAC and gross margin goals.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** KPI targets must be phased according to the release timing of product.

- **Efficiency:** CAC, Payback Period, LTV:CAC ratio.
- **Marketing:** Cost per Lead, MQL-to-SQL conversion, Channel ROI.
- **Sales:** Win Rate, Average Deal Size, Sales Cycle Length, Quota Attainment.
- **Growth:** Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) growth, Velocity.

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Sub-task 1 (Stakeholders):** The ICP must be a refined version of the primary target segments.
- **Phase 7: Market:** Use the SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) to define the scope of the Target Account List.

- **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):** Detailed firmographics and technographics.
- **Target Account List (TAL):** Tiered list of high-priority prospects.
- **Pipeline Management:** Stages, weighted values, and forecasting methodology.

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 6: Sub-task 1 (Traction):** This sub-task expands the high-level traction data into a GTM-specific proof-point repository.
- **Phase 1: Research:** Use founder pedigree and initial research findings as supporting evidence.

Document evidence that the business model works:
- **Traction Metrics:** Revenue trajectory, customer growth, LOIs (Letters of Intent).
- **Track Records:** Previous successes, founder pedigree in the space.
- **Social Proof:** Customer testimonials, case studies, NPS scores.
- **Validation:** Beta results, pilot program outcomes.

### 11. Create GTM Budget

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 5: Business Model:** Reconcile GTM spend with the overall Cost Structure.
- **Phase 13: Financials:** Budget must align with the total available runway and funding rounds.

- **Program Spend:** Ad spend, event costs, content production.
- **Headcount:** Marketing and Sales team compensation (base + variable).
- **Tooling:** Software licenses (CRM, Marketing Automation, etc.).
- **ROI Projections:** Expected revenue yield per dollar of GTM investment.
