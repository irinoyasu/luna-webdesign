# Research Phase Reference

## Purpose

Research the customer deeply, qualify the opportunity, and map their pain points and priorities. This phase ensures you pursue winnable opportunities and enter the strategy phase with genuine customer insight — not assumptions.

**The single most important phase.** Research shows that 57% of B2B buyers have already defined their needs before talking to a vendor (Gartner). Your proposal must demonstrate that you understand their world better than they expect.

### 1. Customer & Market Research

Before anything else, become an expert on the customer. For unsolicited proposals, this replaces RFP analysis entirely — you must discover what an RFP would have told you.

**Inputs:**
- Customer website, annual reports, press releases, social media
- Industry reports and market data
- News articles and regulatory filings
- Any existing materials or prior relationship notes
- RFP document (if RFP response)

2. **Understand Their Strategic Context:**
   - What are their stated strategic priorities? (Source: annual reports, CEO statements)
   - What market pressures are they facing? (Competition, regulation, technology shifts)
   - What are they investing in? (Expansion, modernization, new markets)
   - What problems are they trying to solve? (Public statements, job postings, RFI/RFQ history)

3. **Map the Decision-Making Unit:**
   | Role | Name (if known) | Primary Concern | Communication Style |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | Final decision-maker | | | |
   | Budget holder | | | |
   | Technical evaluator | | | |

4. **Analyze Market & Industry Context:**
   - Market size and growth rate for the relevant segment

   - Technology trends relevant to the opportunity

5. **For RFP Responses — Decompose the RFP:**
   - Map all mandatory requirements by section
   - Categorize: mandatory (M), desirable (D), informational (I)

### 3. Competitive Landscape Deep Search

Do NOT rely on assumptions or general knowledge about competitors. Perform active web research to build a fact-based competitive picture. This is essential for crafting differentiated win themes in the Strategy phase.

**Instructions:**

1. **Identify Competitors (use active web research):**
   Search for companies that the customer might use instead of you. Include:
   - **Direct competitors:** Companies offering the same or similar product/service
   - **Indirect competitors / Alternatives:** Different approaches the customer could take to solve the same problem (e.g., building in-house, using a generalist vendor, maintaining the status quo)
   - **Incumbent vendors:** Who is the customer currently using? (Search news, press releases, trade publications)

### 4. Customer's Customers & Competitors Deep Search

Understanding who the customer sells to and who they compete against is **essential** for crafting a proposal that demonstrates genuine business empathy. This sub-task researches the customer's own market dynamics — not your competitors (that's Sub-Task 4), but the customer's customers and the customer's competitors. This intelligence enables you to:
- Frame your solution in terms of how it helps the customer win against *their* competitors
- Speak the language of the customer's end-users and buyers
- Identify market pressures that create urgency for your proposal

> **⚠️ MANDATORY:** Actively search the web and read source URLs. Do NOT rely on assumptions.

**Inputs:**
- Customer profile (from Sub-Task 1)
- Customer's website, product pages, case studies, press releases
- Industry reports and trade publications

**Instructions:**

1. **Research the Customer's Customers (use active web research):**

   |---|---|

   | Segment size / importance | |
   | What they buy from the customer | |
   | Their key requirements & expectations | |
   | Their procurement process / decision criteria | |
   | Pain points they experience (relevant to our proposal) | |
   | Industry-specific regulations or standards they must meet | |

2. **Research the Customer's Competitors (use active web research):**
   Identify companies that compete directly with the customer in their market.
   | # | Customer's Competitor | Core Products / Services | Market Position | Source URL |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | 1 | | | | |

   For each major competitor, document:
   | Field | Detail |
   |---|---|
   | Company name & website | |
   | Size relative to our customer | Larger / Similar / Smaller |
   | Key strengths vs. our customer | |
   | Key weaknesses vs. our customer | |
   | How they address the same market needs | |
   | What they do better / worse than our customer | |
   | Recent moves (new products, partnerships, market expansion) | |

3. **Analyze Market Pressures on the Customer:**
   Use the customer's competitor landscape to identify pressures that create urgency for your proposal.
   | # | Market Pressure | How It Affects the Customer | How Our Proposal Helps |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | 1 | Competitor X is expanding into customer's territory | Customer risks losing market share | Our solution accelerates customer's market presence / response time |
   | 2 | Customer's customers demand [specific capability] | Customer cannot meet this demand today | Our proposal directly enables this capability |

4. **Frame Your Value in the Customer's Market Context:**
   Translate these findings into proposal messaging:
   | Insight | Proposal Messaging Opportunity |
   |---|---|
   | Customer's customers require [X] | "Your buyers increasingly demand [X]. Our solution ensures you meet this requirement before your competitors do." |
   | Customer's competitor does [Y] better | "To close the gap with [ghosted competitor], this proposal gives you [Y capability] within [timeframe]." |
   | Customer's market is shifting toward [Z] | "The market is moving toward [Z]. Our proposal positions you at the forefront of this shift." |

**Outputs:**
- `research/customer-market-dynamics.md` containing:
  - Customer's customer segments with profiles
  - Customer's competitor profiles with source URLs
  - Market pressure analysis
  - Proposal messaging opportunities derived from market context
