# Purpose Phase Reference

## Purpose

Define the foundational Mission, Vision, and Values. This phase establishes the long-term direction and social purpose of the company to align all stakeholders.

## Inputs

**Process Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity (Mandatory):** The mission must address the core identified problem.
- **Phase 3: Solution (Mandatory):** The vision is the long-term realization of the solution's ultimate value.
- **Phase 11: Team (Mandatory):** Founders' personal values and shared cultural norms from the Team phase must be synthesized.

**You MUST synthesize information from ALL previously completed phases before drafting.** 
The business plan is a cumulative pipeline. Your inputs include:
1. **Previous Phase Outputs:** All documents and research logs generated in Phases 1-11.
2. **Existing Workspace Data:** Always check the output directory (`{output_folder}/businessplan-writing/`) for any existing `purpose/` folder or data from preceding phases. If files already exist from a previous execution, you must incorporate their content to ensure continuity and avoid duplication.
3. **Primary Direct Dependencies:** Phase 3: Solution (ultimate value goal) and Phase 11: Team (shared culture and founder background).

## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Write Mission Statement

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** The mission must be the active force that solves the core identified pain point.
- **Phase 3: Solution:** Integrate the unique "how" or approach into the mission statement.

Define *why* the company exists:
- What does the company do?
- For whom? (target customer)
- How? (unique approach or method)
- What impact does it create?

**Criteria:** Clear, concise (1–2 sentences), inspiring, actionable, differentiating

**Process:**
1. Review problem statement, solution definition, and customer insights
2. Draft 3–5 mission statement candidates
3. Evaluate each against criteria
4. Select and refine the final statement
5. Test with stakeholders

**Example:** "To empower small businesses with AI-driven marketing tools that level the playing field against enterprise competitors."

### 2. Write Vision Statement

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 3: Solution:** The vision is the long-term, idealized state when the solution has achieved its full impact.
- **Phase 7: Market:** Use the market growth potential to scale the ambition of the vision.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** The vision must be the long-term destination starting from the release timing of product.

Define the future state the company aspires to create:
- 5–10 year aspirational goal
- The world that exists when the mission is fully realized
- Ambitious but achievable

**Criteria:** Inspiring, forward-looking, memorable, aligned with mission

**Example:** "A world where every small business has access to enterprise-grade marketing intelligence."

### 3. Write Core Values

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 11: Team:** Use the founders' personal values and shared norms as the basis for organizational values.
- **Phase 1: Research:** Use the company's founding history to identify "imprinted" values.

Define 3–5 core values that guide decisions and behavior. Use the `culture-architect` methodology to ensure they are descriptive, not just aspirational.

**For each value:**
- **Value Name:** Concise and memorable.
- **Definition:** 1–2 sentences explaining the "why."
- **Source of Truth:** Start with observed positive behaviors from the team (e.g., "What did our last great hire do without being asked?").

### 4. Define Behavioral Anchors

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 3 (Core Values):** Anchors must be the direct behavioral translations of the defined core values.
- **Phase 11: Sub-task 4 (Recruitment Policy):** Align anchors with the evaluation criteria used in the interview process.

- **Draft Behavioral Anchors:** For each value, define 2-3 concrete "Dos and Don'ts."
    - **Dos:** Observable actions that demonstrate the value (e.g., "We share bad news within 24 hours").
    - **Don'ts:** Actions that violate the value (e.g., "We don't hide mistakes to protect optics").
- **Verification:** Ask "How would a new hire know we live this on day 30?" If the answer is vague, refine the anchor.
- **Deliverable:** `purpose/behaviors.md` containing the values-to-behaviors translation matrix.

### 5. Create Culture Code

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 4 (Behaviors):** Incorporate the "Dos and Don'ts" into the operating system.
- **Phase 11: Sub-task 1 (Org Chart):** Align ritual frequency and communication norms with the team size and reporting structure.

- **Who Thrives vs. Who Doesn't:**
    - **Who Thrives:** List the specific mindsets and work styles that succeed here.
    - **Who Doesn't Thrive:** Be honest about who would be unhappy (e.g., "If you need a highly structured environment with zero ambiguity, you won't thrive here").
- **Define Cultural Rituals:**
    - **Internal Rituals:** Weekly all-hands, retrospectives, or recognition traditions.
    - **Stage-Appropriate Delivery:** Ensure rituals are designed for the current team size (Seed vs. Growth).
- **Communication & Decision Making:** Document *how* the team communicates (e.g., "default to transparency") and *how* decisions are made.
- **Deliverable:** `purpose/culture-code.md` containing the formal culture code and ritual plan.
