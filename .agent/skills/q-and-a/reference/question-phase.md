Generating a comprehensive set of questions for the business plan by applying the judging criteria.

## Inputs

**Business Plan Deliverables:** Analysis of all directories and files in `businessplan-writing/` folder.

## Persona Strategic Focus

Each persona represents a specific investment thesis. You MUST apply these lenses when generating questions:

1. **Sam Altman (Y Combinator/Moonshot Logic)**: Look for **contrarian truths** and **exponential growth**. Ask "What do you know that others don't?" and "How does this become a \$100B company?"
2. **Seed Stage VC (Build/Traction Logic)**: Look for **operational feasibility** and **entry strategy**. Ask "Why now?" and "How will you survive the first 12 months with no revenue?"
3. **Series B Stage VC (Scale/Economics Logic)**: Look for **unit economics** and **predictable scaling**. Ask "What is your LTV/CAC at 10x scale?" and "Where are the bottlenecks in your organizational structure?"
4. **MIT Business Plan Contest (Academic/Structural Logic)**: Look for **clarity**, **realism**, and **team quality**. Ask "Is the opportunity both attractive and realistic?" and "Is the team of sufficient breadth to make it happen?"

## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### General Principles for All Personas
- **Exact Match for Criteria Count**: The number of questions generated for each persona MUST exactly match the number of criteria listed for that persona. 
    - Sam Altman: 23 questions.
    - Seed Stage VC: 13 questions.
    - Series B Stage VC: 30 questions.
    - MIT Business Plan Contest: 16 questions.
- **One Question per Criterion**: You MUST generate exactly one question for every criterion listed in the "Judging Criteria" section for that persona.
- **Synthesize Before Drafting**: Read the `businessplan-writing/` files thoroughly. Questions must not be generic; they must target specific numbers, claims, or gaps in *this* plan.
- **Challenging Tone**: Questions should simulate a high-stakes interview. Be critical but professional.
- **Output Format**: For each sub-task, save the results to the specified file in `question/` (e.g., `questions-altman.md`) using a Markdown table mapping Criteria to Question.

### 1. Generate Questions (Sam Altman)
- **Goal**: Challenge the core assumptions and "moonshot" potential of the startup.
- **Strategy**: Look for contrarian truths, exponential growth potential, and evidence of founder "grit" and "obsession".
- **Instructions**:
    - Review the 23 Sam Altman criteria.
    - Generate exactly **23 questions** (one per criterion).
    - For each criterion, identify a specific element in the business plan (e.g., in `solution/`, `market/`, or `team/`) that aligns with it.
    - Draft a question that pushes the founder to explain how they meet that specific criterion with evidence.

### 2. Generate Questions (Seed Stage VC)
- **Goal**: Focus on operational feasibility, early survival, and immediate market entry.
- **Strategy**: Ask "Why now?", analyze the MVP path, and scrutinize the competitive landscape for early-stage threats.
- **Instructions**:
    - Review the 13 Seed Stage VC (Incubate Fund) criteria.
    - Generate exactly **13 questions** (one per criterion).
    - Focus on the `problem/`, `solution/`, and `go-to-market/` directories.
    - Draft questions that test the team's ability to survive the first 12 months and achieve initial traction.

### 3. Generate Questions (Series B Stage VC)
- **Focus**: Scalability, unit economics, and organizational readiness for rapid expansion.
- **Strategy**: Scrutinize month-over-month growth, LTV/CAC sustainability, and the strength of the "network effect" or moat.
- **Instructions**:
    - Review the 30 Series B questions/criteria.
    - Generate exactly **30 questions** (one per criterion).
    - Adapt these questions to the specific data found in `financials/`, `traction-progress/`, and `operations/`.
    - Even if the plan is early-stage, ask how they *will* achieve and measure these Series B metrics.

### 4. Generate Questions (MIT Business Plan Contest)
- **Goal**: Structural clarity, academic realism, and team breadth.
- **Strategy**: Evaluate the plan for logical consistency, realistic projections, and the breadth/quality of the founding team.
- **Instructions**:
    - Review the 16 MIT Business Plan Contest questions/criteria.
    - Generate exactly **16 questions** (one per criterion).
    - Evaluate the entire `businessplan-writing/` folder for consistency and professional writing quality.
    - Focus on "defensibility" and the realism of the presented business opportunity.
