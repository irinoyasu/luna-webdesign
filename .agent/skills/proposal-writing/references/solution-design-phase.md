# Solution Design Phase Reference

## Purpose

Design the complete proposed solution — what you will deliver, how you will deliver it, and when. This phase bridges strategy and writing: it produces the concrete solution architecture that subsequent phases will cost, price, and present persuasively.

**Key principle:** The solution must be designed *to address customer pain points*, not to showcase your capabilities. Every element of the solution should trace back to a pain point or priority identified in Research.

> **Note:** Cost estimation, pricing strategy, and ROI are handled in the next phase: **Costing & Pricing**. This phase focuses purely on the *what* and *how* of the solution.

## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Design Solution Architecture

Define the complete proposed solution. This is what you're selling — make it concrete, specific, and clearly tied to the customer's needs.

**Inputs:**
- Customer pain points and priorities (from Research)
- Win themes and value proposition (from Strategy)
- Your capabilities, past projects, available resources
- Industry standards and regulatory requirements

**Instructions:**

1. **Solution Overview:**
   Write a 2-3 sentence summary of what you are proposing. This should be understandable by a non-expert.

3. **Detailed Solution Definition & Traceability:**
   Every solution element MUST trace back to a specific customer problem or opportunity and MUST be defined with a high level of detail. You MUST NOT confuse or combine different solutions. Each distinct solution must have its own separate row in the table, even if they address related problems. (e.g., "30-second AI avatar videos for engagement" and "2-minute loop continuous technical explanation videos" are two entirely different solutions and MUST be placed in two separate rows.) For each solution, define:
   - **Features:** What exactly the solution is or does.
   - **Benefits / 利点:** The value or outcome it delivers to the customer. When defining the "Benefits" (利点), you **MUST** include both a **quantitative metric** (e.g., "$150,000 saved per year", "40% increase in response rate", "200 hours reclaimed") and a **qualitative narrative** (e.g., "empowering the engineering team to focus on high-value closing activities and eliminating process frustration").
   - **Implementer:** Who specifically is responsible for implementing/executing it (e.g., your team role, specific partner, or customer).
   - **Strict Adherence to Source Material:** When mapping solutions from provided inputs (e.g., `proposer_info.md`), you MUST preserve the exact technical nature and terminology of the proposed solution. Do not alter a specific solution (e.g., 'AI Chatbot') into a different concept (e.g., 'Web Diagnostics'). Use the features and benefits exactly as described in the source.

   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | | | | | |

   - Phases, milestones, and timeline
