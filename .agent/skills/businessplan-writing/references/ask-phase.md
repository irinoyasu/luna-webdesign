# Investment Ask Phase Reference

## Purpose

Define the specific fundraising terms and the "Ask" for investors. This phase translates the capital needs into an investment request with clear use of proceeds and milestones. Finalize the investment proposal by defining the specific terms of the fundraising round, the detailed allocation of capital, and the clear milestones that will be achieved. This phase ensures that the business plan concludes with a compelling, actionable "Ask" that provides investors with all the necessary information to proceed with due diligence.

## Inputs

**Process Dependencies:**
- **Phase 13: Financials (Mandatory):** Cash flow trough, funding gaps, and pre-money valuation benchmarks are required to set terms.
- **Phase 15: Execution Planning (Mandatory):** The detailed workstreams and milestones drive the "Use of Proceeds" and "Funding Milestones".

**You MUST synthesize information from ALL previously completed phases before drafting.** 
The business plan is a cumulative pipeline. Your inputs include:
1. **Previous Phase Outputs:** All documents and research logs generated in Phases 1-15.
2. **Existing Workspace Data:** Always check the output directory (`{output_folder}/businessplan-writing/`) for any existing `ask/` folder or data from preceding phases. If files already exist from a previous execution, you must incorporate their content to ensure continuity and avoid duplication.
3. **Primary Direct Dependencies:** Phase 13: Financials (cash flow trough and funding gaps) and Phase 15: Execution Planning (milestones and resource allocation).

## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Define Fundraising Terms

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 13: Financials:** Use the cash flow trough and required funding amount identified in the 120-month projection.
- **Phase 14: Insight:** The valuation cap must reflect the "Why Now" and "Why You" narrative's strategic potential.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Valuation and round timing must align with the release timing of product.

**Goal:** Clearly state the raise size and the mechanics of the investment.

**Process:**
1.  **Raise Size Justification:**
    *   State the **total target amount** (JP/EN).
    *   **Justification:** Explicitly link the size to the **runway** required to reach the next major funding milestone (typically 18-24 months) plus a 20-30% buffer for contingency.
2.  **Investment Instrument:** Select and define the instrument with all necessary parameters:
    *   **Equity (Preferred/Common):** Pre-money valuation, price per share, liquidation preference (e.g., 1x non-participating).
    *   **SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity):** Post-money vs. Pre-money, Valuation Cap, Discount Rate.
    *   **Convertible Note:** Principal, Interest Rate, Maturity Date, Valuation Cap, Discount Rate.
    *   **J-KISS (Keep It Simple Security):** Cap, Discount, and specific Japanese local terms if applicable.
3.  **Minimum/Maximum Participation:** Define the minimum ticket size and any "greenshoe" or over-allotment options.
4.  **Key Rights:** List core investor rights (e.g., Board seat/observer, Pro-rata rights, Major decisions consent).

### 2. Detailed Use of Proceeds

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 13: Financials:** Break down the use of proceeds based on the driver-based OpEx and CapEx assumptions.
- **Phase 15: Execution Planning:** The headcount and program expenses in the use of proceeds must match the personnel hiring and workstream schedules.
- **Phase 11: Team:** Use the recruitment priorities to detail personnel allocation.

**Goal:** Provide transparency on how capital is deployed to create value.

**Process:**
1.  **Category Breakdown (Percentage & Absolute):** Provide a high-level split (e.g., R&D: 45%, GTM: 35%, G&A: 15%, Reserve: 5%).
2.  **Granular Allocation Table:**
    *   **Personnel:** List key hires by function (e.g., "Hire 3 Full-stack Engineers," "Head of Sales").
    *   **Marketing/Growth:** Digital ad spend, event budgets, channel partner incentives.
    *   **Infrastructure/COGS:** Server costs, manufacturing tooling, inventory prep.
    *   **Operations:** Office expansion, legal/audit fees for scale.
3.  **Milestone Linkage:** For every major spend category, explicitly state which **Sub-task 3 Milestone** it directly supports.
4.  **Burn Rate & Runway:** State the projected "Post-funding Monthly Burn" and the "Zero-Revenue Runway" vs. "Projected Revenue Runway."

### 3. Funding Milestones

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 6: Traction:** Use the product/business roadmap as the source for future milestones.
- **Phase 15: Execution Planning:** Align specific due dates with the detailed day-by-day schedule and the release timing of product.

**Goal:** Define the "Value Inflection Points" that de-risk the investment and justify a higher valuation in the next round.

**Process:**
1.  **Categorize Milestones:**
    *   **Product/Tech:** (e.g., "v2.0 Beta Release," "Patent Grant," "SOC2 Compliance").
    *   **Traction/Revenue:** (e.g., "$100k MRR," "10 Enterprise Pilots," "Active Users > 50k").
    *   **Operational/Strategic:** (e.g., "Core Management Team hired," "Distribution partnership with X signed").
2.  **Quantify & Time-bind:** Every milestone must be **SMART** (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).
    *   *Bad:* "Increase revenue."
    *   *Good:* "Achieve $1.5M ARR by Month 18."
3.  **De-risking Narrative:** Explain why reaching these milestones makes the company significantly more valuable (e.g., "Reaching $1M ARR proves repeatable sales motion, reducing GTM risk for Series B").

### 4. Investor Target Profile

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 13: Financials:** The target investor must be appropriate for the round size and valuation stage.
- **Phase 7: Market:** Identify investors with a proven track record or strategic interest in the specific target market.
- **Phase 14: Insight:** Align the investor persona with the "Contrarian Hypothesis" to find believers in the core thesis.

**Process:**
1. Define the **ideal investor persona** (e.g., "Tier-1 VC with SaaS expertise," "Strategic partner in Logistics," "Impact-focused Angel").
2. List **Value-Add requirements** beyond capital (e.g., network access, hiring support, regulatory expertise).
3. Identify **5–10 specific target firms or individuals** and the rationale for each.
4. Note any **investor exclusions** or sensitive relationships.

### 5. Closing Timeline & Next Steps

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 15: Execution Planning:** The closing timeline must be synchronized with the project's cash-out dates and critical path starts.
- **Phase 13: Financials:** Timing must account for current runway and the "drop dead" date for new capital.

**Process:**
1. Map the **fundraising schedule** (e.g., "Term Sheet by Month 1," "Due Diligence Month 2," "Closing Month 3").
2. Provide a **Data Room Checklist** (list of documents ready for review).
3. State the **clear call to action** for investors (e.g., "Schedule a deep-dive on technology," "Request access to data room").
4. Provide **Contact Information** for the primary fundraising lead.
