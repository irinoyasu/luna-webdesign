# Traction and Progress Phase Reference

## Purpose

Document current achievements and map out the future roadmap. This phase demonstrates momentum and builds credibility for the product's development and market adoption.

## Inputs

**Process Dependencies:**
- **Phase 4: Technology (Mandatory):** Developmental milestones and technical debt must be understood before projecting the roadmap.
- **Phase 1: Research (Mandatory):** Historical metrics and past achievements from company background research.

**You MUST synthesize information from ALL previously completed phases before drafting.** 
The business plan is a cumulative pipeline. Your inputs include:
1. **Previous Phase Outputs:** All documents and research logs generated in Phases 1-5.
2. **Existing Workspace Data:** Always check the output directory (`{output_folder}/businessplan-writing/`) for any existing `traction-progress/` folder or data from preceding phases. If files already exist from a previous execution, you must incorporate their content to ensure continuity and avoid duplication.
3. **Primary Direct Dependencies:** Phase 3: Solution (product name, core features, value proposition), Phase 4: Technology (development roadmap, technical milestones), and Phase 5: Business Model (resource availability and revenue generation targets).

## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Write Current Traction

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 1: Research:** Use the historical company background and founding context as the baseline for traction.
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** Link current traction to the solution of identified pains.
- **Phase 9: Go-To-Market:** Use current sales pipeline data and conversion metrics as proof points.
- **Phase 11: Team:** Use key hires and team growth as a qualitative traction metric.

Document the evidence of progress and product-market fit:
- **Quantitative Metrics:** User growth, revenue (MRR/ARR), retention rates, engagement data, CAC/LTV trends.
- **Qualitative Proof Points:** Key partnerships, pilot programs, customer testimonials, case studies, awards/recognition.
- **Product Status:** Version history, current feature set, stability/performance metrics.
- **Sales Pipeline:** Number of qualified leads, conversion rates, average deal size, stage distribution.

### 2. Write Product Roadmap

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 4: Technology:** The product roadmap must be the "market-facing" version of the technical roadmap, ensuring engineering feasibility.
- **Phase 3: Solution:** Use core features and future capabilities identified in the solution design.
- **Phase 5: Business Model:** Roadmap timing must be consistent with resource availability and burnout limits.
- **Phase 15: Execution Planning:** Roadmap must align with the release timing of product.

Define the future of the product/service:
- **Short-term (0–6 months):** Immediate feature releases, bug fixes, UX improvements.
- **Medium-term (6–18 months):** Major new modules, integrations, platform expansions, geographic localization.
- **Long-term (18+ months):** Strategic R&D, new product lines, ecosystem development, advanced AI/automation capabilities.
- **Visual Representation:** List phases with specific goals and technical requirements.

### 3. Write Key Milestones

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 5: Business Model:** Align milestones with break-even targets, gross margin milestones, and funding needs.
- **Phase 4: Technology:** Align with critical path technical deliverables (Architecture, Beta, v1).
- **Phase 7: Market:** Milestones must reflect market entry timing and intended SOM capture.
- **Phase 15: Execution Planning:** Milestones must be sequenced based on the release timing of product.

Define the critical business objectives:
- **Product Milestones:** MVP launch, Version 1.0, key feature completions.
- **Business Milestones:** Reaching break-even, first 100/1000 customers, major funding rounds (Seed, Series A).
- **Operational Milestones:** Key hires, office expansions, international market entry, ISO/security certifications.
- **Regulatory Milestones:** Patent filings, FDA/CE approvals, compliance audits.

For each milestone, document:
- Target date
- Success criteria (KPIs)
- Required resources (budget, headcount)
- Dependencies and risks
