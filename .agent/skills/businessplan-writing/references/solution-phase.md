## Detailed Sub-Task Instructions

### 1. Define Core Solution Capabilities

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** Use the "User Stories" and "To-Be State" as the primary source for functional requirements.
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** Resolve the specific pains identified in the "JTBD" sub-task.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Align feature set with the release timing of product (MVP vs. v1.0).

Define what the product *is* and *does*:
- **Features:** Grouped by category (Core, Differentiating, Future).
- **User Stories:** "As a [persona], I want to [action] so that [benefit]."
- **Product Name & Catchphrase:** 1-2 sentence core message and name selection logic.

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/capabilities.md` detailing the product scope.

### 2. Evaluate Feature ROI

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 3-1 (Capabilities):** Use the finalized feature list for impact/cost analysis.
- **Phase 5: Business Model:** Align feature prioritization with the target profit model and LTV:CAC ratios.

- **Identify Revenue Connection:** Categorize how each feature impacts revenue (Direct Monetization, Retention, Conversion, or Expansion).
- **Assess Cost Structure:** Estimate one-time development costs (team × time) and ongoing COGS/OpEx (hosting, support).
- **Calculate ROI & Payback:** Model Year 1 ROI and the Payback Period (months).
- **Analyze Strategic Value:** Factor in non-financial value such as competitive moats, platform enablement, or risk reduction.
- **Categorize Investment:** Provide a recommendation: **Build Now**, **Build for Strategic Reasons**, **Don't Build (Poor ROI)**, or **Build Later (Need More Data)**.

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/feature_roi.md` containing the ROI analysis and investment recommendations for key features.

### 3. Set Pricing

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 1: Research:** Competitor pricing benchmarks from the research logs.
- **Phase 2: Problem & Opportunity:** WTP is derived from the "Consequences of Problem" and "Failed Attempts" (opportunity cost).
- **Sub-task 3-2 (Feature ROI):** Use the COGS/OpEx per feature to set the minimum profitable price.
- **Phase 6: Traction & Progress:** Pricing strategy must account for the release timing of product and introductory offers.

Define the business model and monetization strategy using the **Value Stick** framework and **Financial Impact Analysis**:

- **Value Stick Mapping:**
    - **WTP (Willingness to Pay):** Estimate the maximum a customer would pay based on identified pains and utility.
    - **Price:** Set the initial target price packages.
    - **Cost:** Estimate COGS and SGA per unit/customer.
    - **WTS (Willingness to Sell):** Minimum acceptable compensation for suppliers/employees (considering non-cash value like mission/equity).
- **Pricing Strategy & Model:** 
    - **Model:** Subscription, usage-based, tiers, or freemium.
    - **Strategy:** Determine if you are "Expanding the Stick" (raising WTP/lowering WTS) or "Slicing the Stick" (capturing more margin).
- **Pricing Impact Analysis (Financial Viability):**
    - **Revenue Impact:** Calculate expected ARPU/ARPA lift.
    - **Churn Risk:** Estimate how price increases affect retention (by segment).
    - **Conversion Impact:** Analyze how pricing/packaging affects trial-to-paid conversion rates.
    - **CAC Payback:** Evaluate the net effect on unit economics (higher ARPU vs. potentially lower conversion).
- **Value Capture Analysis:**
    - **Customer Delight (WTP - Price):** Ensure enough surplus is given to drive adoption/evangelism.
    - **Firm Margin (Price - Cost):** Ensure sustainable profitability.
- **Objection Handling ("Too Expensive" Reframe):** Define if objections are WTP problems (need better product) or Perception problems (need better communication/ROI framing).
- **Pricing Calculation (Final Step):** Consolidate all previous analyses (Value Stick, Impact, and Market Benchmarks) to define the specific price points, tier structures, and billing cycles (e.g., monthly vs. annual). Provide a clear breakdown of "Customer Delight" and "Firm Margin" in numerical terms.
- **Business Domain:** Industry classification and growth paths.

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/pricing.md` detailing the business model specifics, value capture logic, and financial impact assessment.


### 4. Build Value Proposition Canvas

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 2: Sub-task 10 (JTBD):** Use the Jobs, Pains, and Gains directly in the Customer Profile section.
- **Sub-task 1 (Capabilities):** Map the features to "Pain Relievers" and "Gain Creators."

Map the **Customer Profile** (Jobs, Pains, Gains) to the **Value Map** (Products, Pain Relievers, Gain Creators). Identify the "fit" and focus on the most compelling proof points.

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/canvas.md` with visual/tabular fit analysis.

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 1 (Capabilities) & 2 (Feature ROI):** Use the feature list and economic impact data as the baseline for quantification.
- **Phase 2: Sub-task 15 (Consequences):** Quantify value by addressing the specific opportunity costs identified.

- **Economic Value:** Cost savings, revenue uplift, productivity gains (hours saved), risk reduction.
- **Emotional Value:** Confidence, relief, empowerment, and brand association.
- **ROI:** Clear calculation with payback period assumptions.

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/value_impact.md` for financial and strategic justification.

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 4 (Canvas):** Use the identified 'fit' areas to define the primary win messages.
- **Phase 8: Competition:** Win themes must directly contrast with competitor weaknesses.

Win themes are concise statements answering: **"Why should the customer choose us?"**
- **Formula:** [Need] + [Differentiator] + [Quantified Outcome].
- **Proof Points:** Case studies, metrics, or testimonials per theme.
- **Stress Test:** Rebuttals against likely competitor counter-claims.

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/win_themes.md` anchoring the narrative.

**Dependencies:**
- **Sub-task 6 (Win Themes):** Tailor the winning narratives to specific stakeholder fears.
- **Phase 2: Sub-task 1 (Stakeholders):** Map messages to the specific motivations of each decision-maker and blocker.

Tailor messaging to the decision ecosystem:
- **Audience-Specific Messaging:** What convinces the Executive vs. Technical/Financial evaluators?
- **Risk Mitigation:** Addressing the specific fears of blockers (IT Security, Procurement, etc.).

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/playbook.md` for stakeholders and influencers.

**Dependencies:**
- **Phase 8: Competition:** Use direct and indirect competitor analysis to craft "Ghost Competitor" narratives.
- **Sub-task 6 (Win Themes):** Positioning must amplify the primary differentiators.

Address competitor weaknesses indirectly using **Ghost Competitor** messaging (contrast approaches, never name companies).
- **Ghost Statements:** "Unlike approaches that require costly on-site work, our solution is fully remote."
- **"Why Not" Library:** Anticipating objections about status quo or cheaper alternatives.

**Deliverable:**
- `solution/positioning.md` detailing the differentiation strategy.
